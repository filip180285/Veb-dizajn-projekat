      The use of animals in the so-called. scientific and educational purposes is special problem area because it represents a field of still current discussion and divided opinions both among laymen and experts the public. Experimental or experimental animals are used for various purposes:
  • Education
  • Fundamental and applied scientific research
  • Testing the harmfulness of drugs, substances, products
  • Testing and research in the military
    • The most used animals are mice and rats, but also other animal species (dogs, cats, pigs, monkeys, etc.).
Existing European and domestic regulations are based exclusively on the protection of the welfare of experimental animals no rights.

The concept of well-being implies the application of the so-called 3 R rules. 3 The R rule (W. Russel and R. Burch 1959) implies respect for the following principles:     Replacement-replacement of animal models or species depending on the phylogenetic levels     Reduction-reducing the number of experimental animals     Refining-perfecting methods but also improving the conditions in which animals hold

       Alternative models can be:
  • Cell and tissue cultures
  • Mathematical and computer models and programs
  • Existing Databases
A special problem related to experimental animals is the majority attitude of scientists to science without application animal models cannot develop with a simultaneous lack of interest in development alternative. Many experiments are replicated with the explanation that any modification of the experimental design is possible considered a scientifically justified novelty. Also, the overwhelming majority in ethics commissions are made up of scientists themselves. Advocacy for the protection of the welfare of experimental animals can be viewed from the aspect of temporary solutions and current positions until the final abolition of experiments involving animal models.

       On the other hand, advocates of animal rights and total liberation justifiably raise the question of whether on in that way, it only legalizes and justifies the exploitation of animals and thus prolongs it. The connection of certain scientific disciplines (medicine) with the possibility of influencing the health and life of people benefits here position to justify its practice and thus further substantiate it.

       When discussing the justification for using animals in experiments we ask the question ethical and scientific justifications. Unless we are specialists and we believe that members of all species have an equal right to life because they are priority criteria are artificial and based on power, not natural law, then experiments on we consider animals ethically unacceptable. The issue of the assessment of the ethical justification of the examination made by the ethics commissions is especially sensitive, because in practices often mix and equate ethical and scientific justification. Therefore, the ethics of the experiment in practice is assessed exclusively according to how many animals suffer, and not whether is examination at all ethically justified or not. The work on the protection of experimental animals in our country does not have such a long history as it does with others countries.
