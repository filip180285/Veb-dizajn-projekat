      Wild animals are kept in captivity in zoos, circuses, aquariums and dolphinariums but also as pets. The welfare of these animals is seriously endangered because they cannot be provided with natural conditions in which I can to meet their species-specific needs. Zoos primarily serve the entertainment of visitors and the so-called. an aesthetic experience they cannot have priority against the suffering of animals and endangering their interests. So called. the educational role of zoos is not really about "educating" visitors and the public but scientists which is far from the real situation. The so-called the conservation role (conservation of species) is not a viable argument for the survival of modern zoos because that role should be played by reserves. In zoos, animals are actually living exhibits for viewing doomed to life in small cages, beige opportunities to meet their needs for movement, adequate shelter, stimulation. A special problem is the spatial arrangement that leads to the predators and animals that are in nature their prey is found too close which is extra stress.

          Disruption of well-being is especially visible through behavioral disorders so called. stereotypes, self-harm, plucking feathers in birds, etc. By going to zoos you are actually supporting the captivity and exploitation of animals that are there for no reason what it is good for them to give you "pleasure". That is why we need to reconsider our concepts of love for nature and for animals that would it should involve advocating for the protection of their interests.

          Circuses are a special type of animal exploitation that unlike zoo gardens include everyday the practice of neglect, abuse and violence against animals. What is the goal? Fun and profit. Animals in circuses do not perform their points because they are intelligent and because that they are attached to trainers because they are abusive. Dressage involves the use of whips, painful hooks, electricity, prickly collars, beatings. However, agony for these animals it is everyday. When they are not on cruel training, they spend their lives chained in small dirty cages.

          Transports are also particularly problematic because they are often animals they do not receive adequate food for days and water. Veterinary care and assistance are not cost effective. Like circuses, aquariums and dolphinariums serve to make a profit by providing entertainment for spectators. Dressage is based on starvation and swimming pools where dolphins and orcas are kept are a great source of stress cause of small volumes, chlorinated water and the inability of these animals to satisfy their natural Small pools are especially dangerous for dolphins that communicate using sonar, as a result of which these animals they become disoriented and anxious. needs. Self-harm and suicide often occur.
