      Solving the problem of abandoned animals means long-term, systematic work on several levels that implies synchronized implementation of legislation and education. Dogs and cats are domesticated species that have a place next to man and that need his protection and care. Unfortunately, the animals we meet on the streets are just former pets that man has abandoned or their descendants. However, society and citizens pay less attention to reacting to such actions, while humanely treatment with abandoned animals he often encounters disapproval.

Solving the problem of abandoned dogs and cats involves:
  • Responsible ownership (chipping, vaccination, dehelmetization, sterilization)
  • Support for shelters for abandoned animals
  • Foster care
  • CNR or TNR method ("catch, sterilize, release")
The basis for the effective implementation of these elements is the application of the law and the education of citizens. The solution to the very cause of the problem lies in sanctioning irresponsible owners (abandonment animals or its offspring, uncontrolled reproduction, uncontrolled movement of the animal). The existence of a shelter allows a part of the animals to be temporarily (until adoption) or permanently accommodate and take care of, especially animals that are endangered on the street (old, sick, young, unsocialized etc.).

     Adoption is the best way to take care of abandoned animals, but implies strict control fulfillment of conditions and suitability of the owner. For quality adoption, it is not enough that citizens have the desire to adopt an animal, they must educate about the obligations they have to fulfill. This reduces the likelihood that the foster parent will return the animal when problems arise or if he changes his mind, that he will ignore her or leave her. However, due to the large number of abandoned animals, the transitional solution for some dogs and cats is CNR / TNR method which includes: capture of abandoned animals, marking, vaccination, sterilization and return to the location from which they were taken (unless they are sick or aggressive). In order for these animals to survive, they need to have guardians, which is often the case in practice.
